# Art Direction

## Core Vision

Road Trip should immediately be recognizable from a single screenshot.

The visual style must communicate:

- loneliness;
- freedom;
- long-distance travel;
- nostalgia;
- warmth;
- calm.

The player should feel like they are inside an illustrated road movie.

## Visual Reference

Primary reference: **Keep Driving**.

Not because we want to copy it.

Because it perfectly captures:

- cozy atmosphere;
- readable composition;
- emotional lighting;
- minimalist pixel art.

Road Trip should aim for the same emotional quality while having its own
identity.

## Art Style

Style: Modern Pixel Art.

Perspective: Side View.

Camera: Fixed side camera.

The player always watches the road from the side.

## Pixel Rules

Large readable shapes.

Minimal pixel noise.

No unnecessary detail.

Every sprite should be recognizable while driving at full speed.

## Color Palette

Warm colors dominate.

**Morning**
- Soft blue
- Golden sunlight

**Day**
- Natural greens
- Blue sky
- Warm asphalt

**Evening**
- Orange
- Pink
- Purple

**Night**
- Deep blue
- Warm headlights
- Orange windows

**Rain**
- Desaturated colors
- Wet asphalt
- Soft reflections

## Lighting

Lighting creates atmosphere. Not realism.

Golden hour should be the most beautiful time of day.

Rain should completely change the mood.

Night should feel peaceful rather than scary.

## Background Layers

The world is built from multiple scrolling layers.

Sky
→ Clouds
→ Mountains
→ Forest
→ Fields
→ Road
→ Roadside Objects
→ Foreground

Each layer moves at a different speed.

The player constantly feels motion.

## Roads

Roads should never look perfectly clean.

Include:

- patched asphalt
- old lane markings
- small cracks
- road repairs
- different pavement colors

The road tells its own story.

## Vegetation

Vegetation defines regions.

Examples:

- Ural forests
- Birch trees
- Pine forests
- Open fields
- Rolling hills
- Rivers

Every region should be recognizable by vegetation alone.

## Buildings

Buildings are simple. Readable. Grounded.

Examples:

- Gas stations
- Roadside cafés
- Motels
- Villages
- Small shops
- Workshops

No oversized fantasy architecture.

## Cars

Cars are based on real vehicles commonly seen in Russia in 2026.

Silhouettes are more important than tiny details.

Players should recognize a vehicle instantly.

## NPC Design

NPC portraits should feel warm.

Natural expressions.

No exaggerated cartoon faces.

Clothing reflects occupation and region.

## User Interface

Minimal.

The road always occupies most of the screen.

HUD elements are small and unobtrusive.

The phone acts as the primary menu.

## Motion

Everything moves slightly.

- Trees.
- Grass.
- Power lines.
- Clouds.
- Rain.
- Dust.

Motion makes the world feel alive.

## Weather

Weather dramatically changes visuals.

**Rain**
- Wet reflections
- Grey sky
- Mist

**Night Rain**
- Headlight reflections
- Street lights
- Water on asphalt

**Fog**
- Soft contrast
- Limited visibility
- Calm atmosphere

## Visual Goal

Every screenshot should immediately answer: "This is a road trip."

The player should want to keep driving simply because the world is
beautiful.

## Camera Composition

The camera should compose every frame like a road movie.

The car is usually placed in the left third of the screen.

The road leads the eye forward.

Large empty spaces are intentional.

Silence and scenery are as important as gameplay.

The player should often want to take screenshots.
